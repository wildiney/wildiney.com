After 15+ years working at the intersection of design and product, I've noticed something that few PMs with technical or business backgrounds see clearly: **deeply understanding users isn't a competitive advantage — it's a prerequisite.**

## What design teaches that business school doesn't

Design thinking isn't a methodology. It's a way of seeing problems. Before asking "which feature should we build?", it asks "why does the user face this problem?". That shift in perspective seems subtle, but it completely changes the kind of solution that emerges.

## Why this matters for PMs

Product teams with a PM who has design sensibility make faster decisions about UX trade-offs. They know when an experience is "good enough" versus when it's compromising quality perception. And they can defend those decisions with business arguments — not just aesthetic ones.

It's not about PMs knowing how to use Figma. It's about having the vocabulary and empathy to ask the right questions.
